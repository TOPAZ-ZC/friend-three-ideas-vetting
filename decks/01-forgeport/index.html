01 · Core Read
ForgePort

This is real pain, but it is not a cute DAM.

The customer has a genuine fragmentation problem: engineering stores authoritative CAD in one system, marketing stores approved imagery in another, sales enablement assembles dealer packets manually, and distributors receive stale files through email or brittle portals.

The wedge is not "all assets in one place." That is too generic. The wedge is dealer-ready product bundles generated from engineering truth and marketing approval state.

Assay call: build only if the MVP avoids proprietary CAD import at first. The first sellable product should accept STEP/IGES/glTF/OBJ/FBX and attach CAD metadata to dealer bundles. Native SOLIDWORKS/CATIA/NX import is a later paid expansion, not the starting gun.
Why now: channel enablement and 3D commerce pressureWhy hard: PLM gravity
02 · Competitive Reality
Sources S1-S6

The category already exists; the opening is packaging and price.

CompetitorWhat they proveWhat remains open
Unity Asset Manager 3D asset management, RBAC, APIs, SDKs, VPC deployment, previews for FBX/OBJ/AutoCAD/glTF are already mainstream enough for Unity to sell. Unity is strongest when a customer is already in a Unity/real-time 3D workflow. Industrial channel bundles are not the center of gravity.
VNTANA VNTANA directly validates 3D/CAD DAM, PLM/PIM/DAM/eCommerce integration, sales/aftermarket portals, CAD optimization, permissions, and versioning. The opportunity is not absence of product. It is mid-market deployment, narrower ICP, and dealer/distributor workflow depth.
Marketing DAMs Bynder/Canto/Brandfolder own the marketing asset budget and procurement motion. They are not trusted as engineering truth. Their 3D features can be enough for marketing, not for CAD-linked technical bundles.
PLM vendors Windchill/Teamcenter/Arena own the engineering relationship and source-of-truth posture. They are slow, expensive, and rarely beloved by channel marketing. ForgePort should complement, not replace, PLM.
Why nobody built this well at mid-market: the product sits between departments with different budgets, risk tolerance, and definitions of truth. Marketing wants speed. Engineering wants control. Channel ops wants distribution. PLM vendors sell control upward; DAM vendors sell creativity sideways. Nobody wants the messy middle unless the wedge is narrow enough to close.
Do not claim greenfieldClaim workflow ownership
03 · Product Surface
MVP

The MVP is a dealer-bundle machine with CAD previews, not a full PLM replacement.

Module 1

Asset truth

Upload files, metadata, folders, tags, approval state, version history. Accept STEP/IGES/glTF/OBJ/FBX/PDF/JPG/PNG/video at launch.

Module 2

Viewer layer

3D web viewer for GLB/OBJ/FBX/USDZ, CAD preview from neutral files, property extraction where feasible, no promise of perfect native CAD fidelity at MVP.

Module 3

Dealer portals

Branded external portals, bundle builder, permissions, expiring links, request workflows, distributor-specific catalog packs.

05 · Risks
Kill Gates

The risk is not technical impossibility. It is enterprise gravity.

RiskSeverityWhat it meansMitigation
CAD kernel scope creepHighNative CAD import across proprietary formats drags the company into expensive SDKs and support burden.Start with neutral formats and paid pilots; use OCCT for MVP where viable; add HOOPS/CAD Exchanger only after revenue justifies it.
Three-buyer sales motionHighEngineering, marketing, and channel ops all care, but none may own the full budget.Sell to channel ops with engineering read-only safety and marketing approval hooks.
PLM vendor squeezeMediumPLM vendors can claim the CAD relationship and block integration access.Be the publishing layer, not the PLM of record.
