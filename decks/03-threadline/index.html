01 · Why I'd Build It
Conviction · 3 pillars

Three things have to be true for this to be a $250M outcome. All three are.

01

SMS is the only inbox that still gets a 90% open rate.

EZ Texting's 2026 read on the US SMS marketing market: $2.86B in 2023 → $9.96B projected by 2030 (≈19.5% CAGR) [S19]. Subtext shipped 10B messages and 28M subscribers in 2025, grew revenue 105% YoY, and reported sub-1% churn [S14]. The behavioral primitive — "I will read a text from this number" — is not just intact, it is the only direct channel that has not been arbitraged away by algorithmic feeds.

02

No one owns the operator desk for paid premium communities.

Community.com is broadcast-shaped and reads as celebrity/brand-scale [S15]. Subtext is media/audience messaging [S14]. Discord, Circle, Geneva, Mighty Networks, and Skool all bet that members will keep coming back to a feed/room — which is the exact behavior premium-community operators say is decaying. Threadline sits in the gap: a single-pane operator console where the phone inbox is the member surface and the desk is the brain.

03

AI now changes one operator's throughput by 4-6×.

One operator with AI-drafted replies, intent tagging, intro suggestions, and conversation summarization can credibly run a 5,000-person community at the quality bar that previously required 3-4 community managers. That is what shifts the per-customer ACV from $99 SaaS to $799-$5,000. The AI isn't the moat — operator workflow + relationship memory + compliance discipline is. AI is the oxygen that lets one person ride that desk.

My read: the existing community-tools space optimized for two things — content rooms and broadcast SMS. Threadline picks the unloved middle: private, high-context, paid relationships managed through one inbox. The reason no one has shipped this exact product is structural — SMS companies came up through the marketing-automation stack (campaigns, opt-ins, drip flows), and community companies came up through the forum/feed stack (rooms, threads, courses). Neither parent gives birth to "concierge desk." Threadline does.

05 · 90-Day Sprint Plan
How I'd actually build it

Six 2-week sprints. Pilot revenue in week 8. Gross-margin telemetry on day 1.

Sprint 1 · Weeks 1-2

Twilio + Postgres + auth foundation. Single thread end-to-end.

Provision Twilio Messaging Service + 10DLC brand registration + first campaign vetting (3-week lead time, kicks off Day 1). Stand up Next.js 16 + Vercel + Supabase Postgres + Supabase Auth (magic link). Wire one webhook: inbound SMS → message row → operator inbox view. STOP / HELP / quiet hours from day one — non-negotiable for compliance.

Sprint 2 · Weeks 3-4

Member graph schema. Operator desk shell.

Tables: members, threads, messages, tags, events, rsvps, intros, consent_records, cogs_ledger. Operator desk shell: unified inbox, member card with full history, tag editor, segment filters. RLS policies on every table from day one — service-role-only writes for billing and compliance.

Sprint 3 · Weeks 5-6

AI draft + intent tagging. Approval-gated by default.

Anthropic Claude Haiku 4.5 for drafts (cost target <$0.02 per draft). Pinecone or pgvector for member-context retrieval. Every AI output passes through an approve-edit-reject UI before send. Intent tagging runs async on every inbound message. Cost telemetry per customer in the admin pane — operators see daily SMS + AI spend.

Sprint 4 · Weeks 7-8

Event RSVP, drops, intro queue. First 3 paid pilots.

Event RSVP with reply-keyword grammar ("YES", "MAYBE", "NO + reason"). Drops = scheduled segmented broadcasts with included-credit accounting. Intro queue: operator pairs two members, AI drafts the warm intro, operator approves. First 3 paid pilots live by week 8 at $799/mo with white-glove import.

Sprint 5 · Weeks 9-10

Renewal-risk signal. Margin telemetry. Stripe billing.

Renewal-risk score (last-text-recency × event no-show rate × thread-staleness). Stripe Billing for $799/$2,250/$5,500 tiers + usage overage billing on segments above bundle. Per-customer gross-margin dashboard for the operator-of-Threadline (us). Alert if any customer drops below 70% GM for 2 consecutive weeks.

Sprint 6 · Weeks 11-12

10 paid pilots live. Retention baseline. Kill-gate evaluation.

Close 10 paid pilots. Run 30-day retention measurement: (a) operator weekly active > 4× sessions/week, (b) member reply rate > 18%, (c) usage-adjusted GM > 75%. Below those bars on 4 of 10 = re-scope before raising. Above on 7+ of 10 = move to seed raise.

09 · Defensibility
Moat thesis

The moat isn't AI. It's the relationship graph + workflow lock-in + compliance posture.

Moat 01 · Relationship graph

Member memory the operator can't recreate elsewhere.

After 6 months on Threadline, an operator has 1,000+ tagged conversations, 200+ documented intros, 50+ event RSVP histories per member, and renewal-risk signals tied to behavior. Migrating that to a competitor means rebuilding 6-12 months of context. Switching cost is real, not theoretical.

Moat 02 · Workflow lock-in

The operator's daily ritual runs on this desk.

If the morning routine is: open Threadline, work the priority queue, approve 12 AI drafts, fire 4 intros, check renewal risk — that ritual replaces what a CSM team used to do. Replacing the desk means replacing the job. Operators don't switch unless something breaks.

Moat 03 · Compliance posture

10DLC + TCPA done right is a barrier, not a checkbox.

A new entrant has to register a brand, pass campaign vetting (3-6 weeks), implement consent ledger + STOP / quiet hours, and survive their first TCPA letter. Threadline does this on every customer onboarding by default. New entrants who skip it ship faster — and discover compliance debt at the worst time [S13][S17].

Anti-moat reality check: the AI inference itself is not defensible — Anthropic, OpenAI, Google all sell the same cognitive capacity. The product moat is what surrounds the AI: the data shape, the workflow ritual, the compliance ledger, the operator trust. Any "AI for community" pitch that leans on the model as the moat is going to lose to the team that owns the workflow.
AI is fuelWorkflow + memory + compliance is the moat
