01 · Core Read
SceneYield

The insight is strong. The buyer is weak.

The sponsor lifecycle for independent cultural events is badly operated. Producers sell vibe, decks, and relationships, then manually assemble screenshots and attendance notes after the event. Sponsors want attribution, audience fit, opt-in leads, and renewal logic.

That problem is real. The mistake is assuming the producer is a clean SaaS buyer. Many cultural producers are seasonal, undercapitalized, DIY, and allergic to fixed software overhead.

Assay call: do not build this as a standalone subscription product first. Build it only if a sponsor, agency, or event collective funds the reporting layer, or if SceneYield is paired with a service-led sponsor packaging offer.
What works: sponsor attributionWhat breaks: buyer economics
02 · Market + Comp Reality
Sources S7-S11

Event tech splits between free RSVP and enterprise marketing suites.

LayerObserved market signalImplication for SceneYield
Enterprise event OS Bizzabo starts at $17,999/year and includes reporting, CRM, ticketing, mobile app, support, plus sponsor portal as an add-on. Enterprise tools already understand sponsors; SceneYield cannot win by being a smaller Bizzabo.
Free/social RSVP Luma is free with unlimited events/guests; Luma Plus is $59/month annually. Partiful's core event product is free. The producer default is cheap/free. $300-$1,500/month needs direct sponsor ROI, not convenience.
Experiential spend PQ Media reported US experiential marketing at $52.80B in 2023 and US event sponsorship at $19.91B in 2023. The money exists, but much of it is controlled by brands, agencies, venues, sports, and enterprise activations, not small producers.
Splash Splash sells event marketing infrastructure with role licenses, CRM sync, custom templates, badge printing, and brand management. The sponsor layer is adjacent to existing enterprise event marketing. The down-market wedge must be simpler and sponsor-first.
Why Splash or Luma has not shipped this fully: Splash serves enterprise marketers where sponsorship is one module among many. Luma and Partiful win by reducing event friction, not by becoming sponsor finance systems. SceneYield is between both: too monetized for free RSVP, too small for enterprise event suites.

05 · Risks
Kill Gates

This idea dies if the producer has to pay before the sponsor cares.

RiskSeverityWhat it meansMitigation
Cheap buyerHighIndependent cultural producers often optimize for survival, not software leverage.Sell through sponsor reporting, collectives, agencies, or rev-share; avoid pure SaaS first.
SeasonalityHighSponsor revenue comes in waves, making monthly subscription retention fragile.Annual sponsor calendar planning and post-event renewal pipeline, not per-event tools.
Privacy burdenMediumAttendee data, opt-ins, sponsor-injected questions, CCPA/GDPR all create legal complexity.Aggregated clean-room default; explicit opt-in for lead sharing; sponsor gets reports, not raw data by default.
Integration dragMediumBrand sponsors want CRM/martech exports, and producers cannot support custom ops.Start with CSV + HubSpot/Salesforce-lite exports; avoid bespoke sponsor integrations until volume.
Kill gate: if five sponsors will not say "we would pay for this report across a portfolio of events," do not build. Producer enthusiasm alone is not enough.
