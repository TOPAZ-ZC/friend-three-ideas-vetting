One-screen answer

The ranking is not the same as the ceiling.

ForgePort can be worth more if it works. Threadline is the better first build. SceneYield is the one that sounds most culturally sharp but monetizes through the weakest buyer. No Bad Company is a real media business — but the platform thesis attached to it cannot ship without a CTO co-founder.

Threadline Build first Clear owned-audience pain, narrower MVP, obvious operator workflow, plausible AI leverage. $25M-$75M realistic, $150M-$250M upside 10 paid communities, 30-day retention, SMS gross margin above 78%.
ForgePort Build only with design partners Real enterprise pain and high ACV, but CAD fidelity, PLM politics, and three-buyer sales motion make it heavy. $75M-$200M realistic, $300M-$600M upside 3 manufacturers sign paid pilots before proprietary CAD import is built.

SceneYield Park, do not SaaS first Producer pain is real, but producer willingness-to-pay is weak and sponsor requirements pull the product into services. $10M-$35M realistic, $75M-$125M only with sponsor-side network A brand sponsor pays for the reporting layer before producers do.
Bottom line: build Threadline first. Keep ForgePort warm with paid design partners. Park SceneYield until a sponsor funds it. Park the No Bad Company platform — but scale the No Bad Company newsletter aggressively because that is the actual asset hiding in the brief. Four ideas, four distinct calls: one fast wedge, one high-ceiling slog, one good insight trapped in a bad buyer, and one real media business with a software fantasy that's pulling the founder away from the business that's actually working.
