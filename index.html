One-screen answer

The ranking is not the same as the ceiling.

ForgePort can be worth more if it works. Threadline is the better first build. SceneYield is the one that sounds most culturally sharp but monetizes through the weakest buyer.

Company Assay Call Why Likely Value Band First Proof Gate
Threadline Build first Clear owned-audience pain, narrower MVP, obvious operator workflow, plausible AI leverage. $25M-$75M realistic, $150M-$250M upside 10 paid communities, 30-day retention, SMS gross margin above 78%.
ForgePort Build only with design partners Real enterprise pain and high ACV, but CAD fidelity, PLM politics, and three-buyer sales motion make it heavy. $75M-$200M realistic, $300M-$600M upside 3 manufacturers sign paid pilots before proprietary CAD import is built.
SceneYield Park, do not SaaS first Producer pain is real, but producer willingness-to-pay is weak and sponsor requirements pull the product into services. $10M-$35M realistic, $75M-$125M only with sponsor-side network A brand sponsor pays for the reporting layer before producers do.
Bottom line: build Threadline first, keep ForgePort as a larger but heavier enterprise bet, and do not build SceneYield as a standalone SaaS until sponsor-side demand is proven. If your friend expects the tool to distinguish all three, this packet does that: one is a fast wedge, one is a high-ceiling enterprise slog, one is a good insight trapped in a bad buyer.
